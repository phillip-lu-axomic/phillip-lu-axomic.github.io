Template 0 - The empty template
In the beginning, there was nothing...

Context:

Ding!

A ticket was just assigned to you. As you click through the ticketing system, you find your heartbeat speed up ever so slightly.

"Please don't be a templating ticket, please don't be a templating ticket..."

"Service::Template::InDesign" stares back at you from the computer screen, as still as the void in your dreams.

You resigned yourself to fate. You readied yourself for the difficult act of leaving another ticket alone for another year when a quick glance to the left of the ticket type left you positively surprised. Instead of the terrifying name of Behemoth Inc., you find a previously unheard of name: Critter Collective. Perhaps this template won't be so bad after all.

You click through the ticket. The entire request reads as follows:

Due to recent rebranding efforts, we would like some templates that are heavily inspired by minimalism. Please create project-based templates for us, with only the project title in it, nothing else. Attached is a sample.

You click on the attached PDF. It looks like a baby made the reference entirely within Paint.

Just a title? In a single page? This might be one of the few templates you can do, even though it's been quite a while since you've touched templating scripting. Let's see if you still remember how to do it...

Request:

Create a custom InDesign template with the following elements:

  • 1 page per project
  • Project-based
  • Name of the project goes near the top half of the page

Actions:

Okay! Let's start making the template!

...

... Except you don't really remember much about what a template really is...Scripts? Templates? Honest you've forgotten what those words even mean.

You try to recall the presentation made by sales that showcased the process of creating a template to potential clients. It went a little something like...

"This is our instance of OpenAsset", the sales pitch would go."Fully cloud-based software designed from the ground up to manage your digital assets!"

"Let's check out the projects that we have in OpenAsset. Let's click here..."

"... and voila! We're in project view. Wow these are really nice projects. In fact, these projects look so nice, I want to show these projects to other people! Let me just select these three, espeically slick looking projects..."

"... and let's populate one of our InDesign templates with these projects! To do so, we'll need to mouse over the InDesign template icon and populate the template we're interested in..."

"Confirm that we want to populate the template."

"... and wow! Looks like the template was created already! Let's take a look at the template."

"Wow... that's clean."
