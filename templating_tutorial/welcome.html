Someone whispers at you from behind - it's the customer success manager. Though the sounds brush against your eardrums at barely above perceptible levels, the impact carries a heavy weight, easily sending your brain into fight or flight response.

"Did you work on the templates from Behemoth Inc. yet?"

You have to be honest. You haven't done anything about the template, and the ticket's been sitting in your queue for close to half a year now. It's not like you can actually do anything about it either: Behemoth Inc. is notorious for their absurd and outrageous template requests. "Multiple Master Pages", would read one request. "3 spreads, 2 pages on first, 5 pages on second, and 9 pages on the last" for another. The requests are as arbitrary as they come.

"I haven't. I've been busy working on client debugging tickets."

The manager scoffs and returns to their workstation. Who hasn't been?, they probably thought.

You let out a quick sigh. What's an engineer like you supposed to do? You glance at the tickets that are assigned to the others. Looks like they're swamped with tickets and ridiculous templates as well: no help from them. Guess there's only one thing you can do:

Take it one template at a time.
